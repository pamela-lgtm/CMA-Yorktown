Market context — Leesburg rental landscape
Leesburg is a genuinely diverse market. Comps here are directional, not prescriptive. What this market does tell us clearly is that well-priced properties are leasing at or above asking price, and the subject's outdoor features — deck and tree canopy — are distinctive in a market where most comparable rentals lack them.

Summary of findings
A healthy market with room to test — and good reason to
Every confirmed lease in this dataset closed at or above asking price, with an average SP/LP of 100.3%. This is not a market where owners need to discount. Leesburg's diversity means there is no single "right" price for this product — the market rewards well-presented properties that are priced thoughtfully. With only one active competing listing (at $2,000, not yet leased), the subject has space to test the ceiling without significant risk of being ignored.
$1,895 is the right launch — it is supported by data and the week-one test is low risk
Katelin Cir — a brand-new 2023 build, 199 sq ft larger — leased at exactly $1,895 in 3 days. That confirms active demand at this price in the current market. Richey Rd, a 3/2 with 95 additional square feet, leased at $1,900 in 18 days. The subject is smaller but brings a distinctive outdoor package — large deck, tree-canopied yard, show-ready — that none of the comparables offer. $1,895 is not a stretch; it is a disciplined test with a clear fallback if needed.
$1,825 is the floor — not a starting point
Goldie St in Sleepy Hollow — a 3/2 at 1,290 sq ft, similar size to the subject, newer build — leased at $1,825 after 94 days. That extended DOM reflects a property that was likely overpriced initially and took time to find its level. At $1,825, the subject has a genuine advantage: its outdoor features, yard character, and show-ready condition make it a more compelling option than a standard 2005 build at the same price. If week one requires a reduction, $1,825 should perform well within the 14–21 day window.
